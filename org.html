WHAT WWF IS DOING

Conserving wildlife is at heart of our mission. We focus on protecting populations of some of the world’s most ecologically, economically, and culturally important species—the survival of which are threatened by poaching, illegal trade and habitat loss. We use the best science available to link on-the-ground work with high-level policy action to create lasting solutions that benefit wild animals as well as the people that live alongside them.

Organizations Working For the betterment of Animal Consrevation

World Wildlife Fund (WWF)

Country: International (headquartered in the United States)

Description: The World Wildlife Fund is a global organization dedicated to wildlife conservation and environmental protection. It operates in various countries around the world and focuses on preserving biodiversity, protecting endangered species, and promoting sustainable practices.

David Shepherd Wildlife Foundation (DSWF)

Country: United Kingdom

Description: The David Shepherd Wildlife Foundation is a UK-based organization committed to wildlife conservation. It supports a range of projects across the globe, aiming to protect endangered species, combat illegal wildlife trade, and raise awareness about the importance of preserving natural habitats.

African Wildlife Foundation (AWF)

Country: United States (headquartered), works extensively in Africa

Description: The African Wildlife Foundation is an international organization with a focus on the conservation of African wildlife and ecosystems. It collaborates with local communities and governments to implement conservation strategies, address habitat loss, and combat poaching, aiming to ensure a sustainable future for Africa's unique biodiversity.
